History of Vedic Mathematics

Vedic Mathematics is a system of mathematics that was rediscovered from the ancient Indian texts called the Vedas, which date back to 1500 BCE or earlier. The term "Vedic" refers to this Vedic basis.

The system was rediscovered by Sri Bharati Krishna Tirthaji (1884-1960), who was the Shankaracharya of Govardhana Matha in Puri, India. According to Tirthaji, all of mathematics is based on sixteen sutras (aphorisms) and thirteen sub-sutras.

These sutras describe natural ways of solving a whole range of mathematical problems in a simple and easy-to-remember format. Vedic Mathematics is said to be based on the working of the natural mind and offers a fresh approach to mathematics.

Unlike conventional mathematics which is linear in approach, Vedic Mathematics offers multiple calculation methods for the same problem, allowing individuals to choose the method that works best for them.

Why Vedic Mathematics?

Mental Calculation

Vedic Math techniques enable complex calculations to be done mentally, without the need for paper or calculator.

Faster Results

Calculations can be performed much faster compared to conventional methods, saving time and effort.

Improves Creativity

The multiple approaches to problem solving encourage creative and flexible thinking.

Easy to Learn

Vedic Math is based on simple rules and principles that are easy to understand and apply.
